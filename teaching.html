Spring 2022

Derived Algebraic Geometry and Shimura Varieties

Course Description & Syllabus

This graduate course explores the intersection of derived algebraic geometry and the theory of cycles, specifically focusing on their applications to the geometry and cohomology of Shimura varieties.

Key Topics:

  • Construction of $\infty$-categories and the Yoneda lemma.
  • The process of animation and animated commutative algebra.
  • The cotangent complex, deformation theory, and obstruction theory.
  • Quasi-smooth schemes, subschemes, and the construction of cycle classes.

Primary References & Reading
  • [Lur09] J. Lurie, Higher Topos Theory. The most complete reference for the basic theory of $\infty$-categories.
  • [Lur17] J. Lurie, Higher Algebra. Foundations of stable $\infty$-categories, the Dold-Kan correspondence, and symmetric monoidal $\infty$-categories.
  • [Cis19] D.-C. Cisinski, Higher Categories and Homotopical Algebra. A more accessible and distinct perspective on the construction and properties of $\infty$-categories.
  • [MG19] A. Mazel-Gee, A user's guide to co/cartesian fibrations. A primer on the Grothendieck construction and its generalization to $\infty$-categories.
  • [MG16] A. Mazel-Gee, UC Berkeley Thesis. Chapter 0 provides a well-written paradigm of $\infty$-categorical thinking.
  • [Mao20] Y. Mao, Revisiting derived crystalline cohomology. A clear exposition on the process of animation and related calculations.
  • [Kha16] A. Khan, Descent in algebraic K-theory. The first few lectures provide a nice introduction to the basics of derived algebraic geometry.
